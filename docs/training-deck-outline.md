## Slide 4: Creating a Form

### Content
Step-by-step with screenshots:

1. Go to **forms.microsoft.com**
2. Click **"New Form"**
3. Add a title and description
4. Click **"Add new"** to add questions
5. Choose your question type (Choice, Text, Rating, Date)
6. Toggle **"Required"** for must-answer questions
7. Click **"Preview"** to test your form

`[Screenshot: Forms home page with "New Form" button highlighted]`
`[Screenshot: Example form with a mix of question types]`

### Speaker Notes
> Let me show you how easy it is to create a form. You go to forms.microsoft.com and sign in with your regular work account. Click "New Form," give it a name, and start adding questions. [DEMO: Create a sample form live with 3-4 questions.] You can choose from different question types — Choice is great for yes/no or multiple choice, Text for open-ended answers, Rating for satisfaction scores, and Date for things like visit dates. ## Slide 8: Tips for Great Forms

### Content
Best practices:

- ✅ **Write clear, simple questions** — ask them the way you'd say them out loud
- ✅ **Use Choice questions when you can** — they're easier to analyze than free-text
- ✅ **Mark critical questions as Required** — so nothing important gets skipped
- ✅ **Keep forms short** — shorter forms get more and better responses
- ✅ **Use consistent naming** — "Clinic A — Intake — 2024" is better than "my form 2"
- ✅ **Preview before sharing** — always test your form first

### Speaker Notes
> Here are a few quick tips to help you create better forms. First, write questions the way you'd say them out loud — keep them clear and simple. Second, use Choice questions (like multiple choice or yes/no) whenever you can, because they're much easier to analyze in charts than free-text answers. Third, mark important questions as Required so people can't skip them. Fourth, keep your forms short — nobody likes a 50-question survey. Fifth, use a consistent naming convention, especially if your team has multiple forms. And finally, always preview your form before sending it out to catch any mistakes.
